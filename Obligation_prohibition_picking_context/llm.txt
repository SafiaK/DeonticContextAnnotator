IT IS OBLIGATORY  
FOR a person  
TO attend a family mediation information and assessment meeting  
BEFORE making a relevant family application  
------------------------  
IT IS PERMITTED  
FOR Family Procedure Rules  
TO provide for subsection (1) not to apply  
WHEN circumstances specified in the Rules  
------------------------  
IT IS PERMITTED  
FOR Family Procedure Rules  
TO make provision about convening a family mediation information and assessment meeting, or about the conduct of such a meeting  
------------------------  
IT IS PERMITTED  
FOR Family Procedure Rules  
TO make provision for the court not to issue, or otherwise deal with, an application  
IF the applicant has not attended a family mediation information and assessment meeting in contravention of subsection (1)  
------------------------  
IT IS PERMITTED  
FOR Family Procedure Rules  
TO provide for a determination as to whether an applicant has contravened subsection (1)  
AFTER considering only evidence of a description specified in the Rules  
------------------------  
IT IS PROHIBITED  
FOR a person  
TO instruct a person to provide expert evidence for use in children proceedings  
WITHOUT the permission of the court  
------------------------  
IT IS PROHIBITED  
FOR a person  
TO cause a child to be medically or psychiatrically examined or otherwise assessed for the purposes of the provision of expert evidence in children proceedings  
WITHOUT the permission of the court  
------------------------  
IT IS PROHIBITED  
FOR a person  
TO put expert evidence (in any form) before the court in children proceedings  
WITHOUT the permission of the court  
------------------------  
IT IS PERMITTED  
FOR the court  
TO give permission as mentioned in subsection (1), (3) or (5)  
ONLY IF the court is of the opinion that the expert evidence is necessary to assist the court to resolve the proceedings justly  
------------------------  
IT IS PERMITTED  
FOR the court  
TO have regard to specific factors  
WHEN deciding whether to give permission as mentioned in subsection (1), (3) or (5)
