IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the driver of a taxi or private hire vehicle which has been hired—
(a) by or for a disabled person, or
(b) by another person who wishes to be accompanied by a disabled person.
or for the driver of a taxi or private hire vehicle if—
(a) the vehicle is being used to provide a local service (within the meaning of section 2 of the Transport Act 1985), and
(b) a person within subsection (1)(a) or (b) has indicated to the driver that the person wishes to travel on the service.
TO (a) to carry the passenger;
(b) if the passenger is in or has with them a wheelchair, to carry the wheelchair;
(c) if the passenger has with them any mobility aids, to carry the mobility aids;
(d) to take such steps as are reasonable to ensure that the passenger is carried in safety and reasonable comfort;
(e) to give the passenger such mobility assistance as is reasonably required;
(f) not to make, or propose to make, any additional charge for complying with a duty mentioned in paragraphs (a) to (e).
UNLESS (a) the taxi or private hire vehicle is designated, and
(b) the disabled person is in a wheelchair
UNLESS more than one wheelchair on any one journey is carried and the vehicle is not of a description prescribed by the Secretary of State
UNLESS a person is carried in circumstances in which it would otherwise be lawful for the driver to refuse to carry the person.
UNLESS (a) an exemption certificate issued to the driver is in force, and
(b) the prescribed notice of the exemption is exhibited on the taxi in the prescribed manner.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR the driver of a taxi or private hire vehicle which has been hired—
(a) by or for a disabled person, or
(b) by another person who wishes to be accompanied by a disabled person.
or for the driver of a taxi or private hire vehicle if—
(a) the vehicle is being used to provide a local service (within the meaning of section 2 of the Transport Act 1985), and
(b) a person within subsection (1)(a) or (b) has indicated to the driver that the person wishes to travel on the service.
TO fail to comply with a duty imposed on the driver by this section
UNLESS (a) the person could not reasonably have known that the passenger was disabled or
(b) it would not have been possible for the wheelchair or mobility aids to be carried safely in the vehicle, or
(c) it would not otherwise have been reasonable in all the circumstances for the wheelchair or mobility aids to be carried in the vehicle or
(d) at the time of the alleged offence the person could not reasonably have known that the passenger required mobility assistance of the type required by the passenger.

(b) a person within subsection (1)(a) or (b) has indicated to the driver that the person wishes to travel on the service
TO (a) to carry the passenger while in the wheelchair;
(c) if the passenger chooses to sit in a passenger seat, to carry the wheelchair;
(ca)if the passenger has with them any mobility aids, to carry the mobility aids;
(d) to take such steps as are necessary to ensure that the passenger is carried in safety and reasonable comfort;
