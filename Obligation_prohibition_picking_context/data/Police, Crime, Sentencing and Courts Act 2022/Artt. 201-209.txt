IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the first regulations made under section 85A(8) of the Courts Act 2003 (as inserted by section 198(1))
TO be made without a draft of the instrument containing them having been laid before and approved by a resolution of each House of Parliament (notwithstanding section 108(3) of the Courts Act 2003).
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the statutory instrument containing the first regulations made under section 85A(8) of the Courts Act 2003 (as inserted by section 198(1))
TO be laid before Parliament
AFTER being made
WHEN/IF/WHERE regulations are made in reliance on subsection (2).
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The Secretary of State
TO by regulations made by statutory instrument make provision that is consequential on this Act.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Regulations under subsection (1)
TO , in particular, amend, repeal or revoke any enactment passed or made before, or in the same Session as, this Act.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Regulations under subsection (1)
TO (a) make different provision for different purposes;
(b) make transitional, transitory or saving provision.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A statutory instrument containing (whether alone or with any other provision) regulations under subsection (1) which amend, repeal or revoke primary legislation
TO not be made
AFTER a draft of the instrument has been laid before, and approved by a resolution of, each House of Parliament.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Regulations under subsection (1)
TO appoint different days for different purposes or areas.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The Secretary of State
TO by regulations made by statutory instrument make transitional, transitory or saving provision in connection with the coming into force of any provision of this Act.
